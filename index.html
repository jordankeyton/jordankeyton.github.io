Intern with the BBC!

The BBC currently have two paid work placement schemes open across BBC Radio 1 and 1Xtra!

This 8 week, paid placement is an entry level opportunity in London for anyone who has a passion for radio, broadcasting and the music and culture of Radio 1 or 1Xtra. You’ll learn audio production skills at BBC Radio 1 or 1Xtra and will gain a 360 experience across live shows and core teams such as music, social media and podcasts. Please note these are off-air schemes, so are not for someone who wants to be a presenter. There are two spaces available for each scheme.
 
Apply via the BBC Careers site. Applications close midnight on 14th June.

Interesting tidbits

Last month, Daft Punk's final, platinum award-winning album, Random Access Memories, turned 10. RAM is where you'll find chart-topper Get Lucky (featuring Pharrell Williams and Nile Rodgers), along with Give Life Back to Music and Doin' it Right (featuring Panda Bear). If you're yet to listen to this album all the way through, there's no better time than now. And once you're done with that, you can also listen to their celebratory 10th Anniversary Edition of RAM, which includes some unreleased demos and other goodies.

In a nice link to my AI tidbit last time, Spotify has launched a new AI DJ feature that can mix tracks together like a human DJ. The feature is called “DJ Mode” and it uses machine learning algorithms to create seamless transitions between songs. It also allows users to customise the mix by selecting different genres and moods.

The best bit (in my opinion) is that there is an actual DJ voice that talks to you. Every few songs it chimes in just to randomly tell you its going to switch up the genre or tell you about a song you used to listen to. I think its sick, but a lot of people think its cheesy. You can read about it below or try it if you have the spotify app. Let me know what you think (and if you think we at Cam FM should be worried...).
